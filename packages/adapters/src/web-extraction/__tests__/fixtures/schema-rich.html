How to Replace a Bike Tyre

Replacing a flat bike tyre is one of the most useful skills a cyclist can have. This guide covers the complete process in 8 clear steps.

What You Need

You will need: tyre levers (×2), replacement inner tube, pump, and optionally a patch kit.

Step 1 — Remove the Wheel

Release the quick-release lever or unscrew the bolt securing the wheel. Lift the wheel clear of the dropouts.

Step 2 — Deflate the Tyre

Press the valve centre pin to release any remaining air pressure.

Step 3 — Remove the Tyre Bead

Insert the curved end of a tyre lever between the tyre bead and the rim. Lever the bead over the rim edge. Repeat around the full circumference.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a tyre replacement take?
Most cyclists complete a tyre replacement in 15–20 minutes.
Do I need tyre levers?
Tyre levers are strongly recommended for tight-fitting tyres.
